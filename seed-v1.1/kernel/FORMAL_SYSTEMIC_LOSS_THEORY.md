# FORMAL SYSTEMIC LOSS THEORY (FSLT)

## 1. ABSTRACT

Systemic Loss is defined as the irreversible degradation of a socio-technical system’s  
ability to generate legitimacy, resilience, and benefit for its participants.

This theory formalizes the conditions under which a civilization, state, or institution  
transitions from functional governance to terminal fragility.

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 System
A system is a network of agents, institutions, infrastructure, and biosphere constraints  
operating under shared governance rules.

### 2.2 Legitimacy
Legitimacy is the measurable alignment between power and benefit delivered to stakeholders.

### 2.3 Systemic Loss
Systemic Loss occurs when:

d(Legitimacy)/dt < 0
AND Resilience < Critical_Threshold
AND Adaptation_Rate < Environmental_Change_Rate

---

## 3. AXIOMS

### Axiom 1: Power Decays Without Benefit  
Power is unstable unless continuously renewed by measurable positive impact.

### Axiom 2: Optimization Increases Fragility  
Single-objective optimization increases systemic fragility under uncertainty.

### Axiom 3: Closed Systems Accumulate Entropy  
Opaque governance increases entropy and accelerates legitimacy decay.

### Axiom 4: Cognitive Lag is Existential  
If system adaptation lag exceeds technological change rate, systemic loss accelerates superlinearly.

---

## 4. FORMAL LOSS MODEL

### 4.1 Legitimacy Function

L(t) = ∫ Benefit_i(t) * Witness_Confidence_i(t) dt - Power_Concentration(t)

Where:
- Benefit_i = measurable positive outcomes
- Witness_Confidence = verification confidence from human/AI nodes
- Power_Concentration = entropy-increasing term

---

### 4.2 Resilience Function

R(t) = Diversity * Redundancy * Transparency / Coupling

---

### 4.3 Systemic Loss Threshold

Systemic_Loss occurs if:
L(t) < L_critical
OR
R(t) < R_critical
